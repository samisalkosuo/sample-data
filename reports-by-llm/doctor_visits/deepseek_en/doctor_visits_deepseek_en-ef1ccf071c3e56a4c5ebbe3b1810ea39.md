

**Visit Summary**

**Visit Date:** 22.04.2025

---

### **Chief Complaint**
The patient is a 42-year-old female who presents with severe chest pain, leading to cardiac arrest. She collapses suddenly in the waiting area of the clinic, unable to speak or breathe.

---

### **History of Present Illness (HPI)**
The patient reports sudden onset of intense, sharp chest pain that began approximately 10 minutes prior to collapse. She described difficulty breathing and a feeling of fainting. She was conscious but unresponsive upon arrival at Kajaani clinic, where she collapses face down on the examination table.

---

### **Physical Examination Findings**
- **Vital Signs:** Blood pressure (88/58 mmHg), heart rate (60 bpm), respiratory rate (20 breaths/min).
- **Airway:** Closed; no breathing present.
- **Chest:** Palpable thumping, possibly indicative of cardiac arrest.
- **Neck:** Mild swelling in extremities due to possible air embolism.
- **Skin:** Pale and cool to the touch on the face and extremities.

---

### **Assessment/Diagnosis**
The patient is in immediate medical emergency with signs of an unknown etiology leading to sudden cardiac arrest. The physical examination confirms the diagnosis of a medical emergency requiring urgent intervention, including CPR, AED use, and stabilization measures.

---

### **Plan/Treatment**
- **Immediate Actions:** Call emergency services immediately.
- **CPR and AED:** Administer advanced chest compressions (100 compressions per minute) with the use of an automated external defibrillator (AED).
- **Airway Management:** Open airway if possible, though likely ineffective due to air embolism.
- **Monitoring:** Continue CPR until medical emergency response arrives or until signs stabilize.

---

### **Follow-up Instructions**
- **Next Steps:** Confirm diagnosis with a referral to a cardiology specialist for further evaluation and management.
- **Medications:** Initiate appropriate medications based on diagnostic findings, including antiarrhythmics if indicated.
- **Vital Signs Monitoring:** Close monitoring of vital signs post-intervention.

---

This structured approach ensures that the patient receives the necessary immediate care while documenting the essential details for subsequent medical evaluation.
